About Deb Johnston

A Cornish girl through and through, Deb has spent the past 20 years gigging in and around Cornwall and has been fortunate enough to have worked with and alongside some of the finest musicians and producers in the County.

Up until the end of 2014 Deb was predominantly known for her drumming, and although she was often seen/heard singing from behind her drum-kit, she liked to remain at the back of the stage.

While singing had always been something Deb had enjoyed, it was not something she found easy to do in front of an audience, or in front of anyone for that matter. For many years Deb was encouraged by close friends and family to do more with her voice, but held back by nerves she could not find the confidence to make the transition to the front of the stage…...until recently.

About Deb Johnston Music

Deb is currently gigging in two different line-ups, both with an acoustic vibe but both totally different acts…...

The Deb Johnston Trio

Backed by a bass player and a cajon/percussionist, Deb covers everything from modern tracks by the likes of Jason M'Raz and Jessie J to Country classics by Dolly Parton & Tammy Wynette. The trio are acoustic but at the same time create the feel of a live band without being overly loud. Deb's charisma and ability to interact with her audience makes the trio a great evening's "entertainment" and something to watch as well as listen to

A Little Twisted Duo

Back in the late 90's Deb drummed for local band Twisted. The band gigged for several years and earned themselves the reputation of being one of the County's finest cover bands. Twisted was fronted by Traci Watts (now Parsons) - a strong singer with an un-canny resemblance to Alanis Morissette. When Twisted dis-banded in 2003 Deb and Traci lost contact, but through the power of social media they got back in contact in 2014. After meeting up and having an impromptu jam session it was clear that they still had a great musical connection. Deb and Traci decided to put together an acoustic set and put on a couple of local gigs....for old time's sake... but the gigs went so well that they have decided to make it a bit more of a regular thing. Their set contains a number of songs that the girls used to gig back in the 90's by the likes of Alanis Morissette, Skunk Anansie, Sheryl Crowe & The Cranberries as well as some more up-to-date material too. With 2 strong voices and great harmonies the duo has been a big hit with audiences of all ages.
